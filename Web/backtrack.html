What is a backing track?

Backing tracks are a recorded musical accompaniment tool, usually consisting of only the rhythm secion, or instrumental recordings of songs excluding specific instruments, so that musicians can play along to with that specific instrument. In this instant, many backing tracks for guitars involve the drums, bass, and piano to accompany for guitarists to play along, and some cases, also the vocals if the song is a specific song and not a repeating chord progression.

Here are many sources on youtube you can find your backing tracks on:

Sebastien Zunino: Youtube channel primarily sharing backing tracks for improvisation on different genres and moods such as blues, latin groove, funk, and so much more!
TGuitar: Youtube channel consisting of mainly improvisation backing tracks for the pop rock genre. If you are practising more pop rock solos, this channel is useful for you!
Guitar Improvisation: A youtube channel consisting of backing tracks for jazz standards, but also just simple jazz progression backing tracks. This channel is useful for people learning jazz guitar, and learning how to improvise through specific loved standards in different tempos and speeds to match your learning pace, or just for jamming in general, if using chord progression charts.
EssentialBackingTracks: Youtube channel that shares smooth, soul, jam tracks in different keys, different progressions and slow to fast tempo for easy practice.
Samurai Jam Tracks: Youtube channel that shares neo soul, city pop, jazz fusion, soul rnb, and much more jazz fusion-genred backing tracks in different keys. This account is really useful in terms of finding different voicing (ways to play the same chord), as it displays on the screen, as well as the fretboard's scale overview to improvise over, making it easy for self practice and revision.

There are so many more backing tracks available on youtube, and also online. To find the backing track of a song you wish to play, searching it up on youtube then adding 'backing track' at the end usually can give you great results.
